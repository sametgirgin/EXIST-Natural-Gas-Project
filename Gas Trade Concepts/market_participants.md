#### 1. SGM Participation (Spot Gas Market)
The Spot Natural Gas Market (SGM) is the market for "Day-Ahead" trading.
- What it means: If a company has "SGM Participation" status, they are authorized to trade gas for delivery on the next day (D+1).
- The Goal: Balancing. If a supplier realized today they sold more gas than they have, they go to the SGM to buy the difference.
- Requirement: To have this status, the entity must have a valid Wholesale or Import/Export license from EPDK (EMRA) and have signed the Spot Pipe Gas Market Participation Agreement.

#### 2. FGM Participation (Futures Gas Market)
The Futures Natural Gas Market (FGM)—which corresponds to the GFM in your screenshot—is for long-term trading.
- What it means: This status allows a participant to trade standardized contracts for future delivery (Weekly, Monthly, Quarterly, Yearly).
- he Goal: Price Risk Management (Hedging). A power plant might use their FGM participation to lock in a gas price for next winter to avoid a price spike.
- Key Difference: Unlike the Spot market (SGM), FGM participation usually requires higher collateral (guarantees) because the contracts last much longer, creating more financial risk.

#### 3. Legal Entity Status
In the EPİAŞ system, "Legal Entity Status" refers to the functional category of the company. In the gas market, not all companies are the same. This status usually falls into one of these categories:
- Wholesale Company: Buys gas from producers/importers and sells to eligible consumers or other retailers.
- Import/Export Company: Brings gas into Turkey (via pipeline or LNG) or sends it abroad.
- Distribution Company: Responsible for the local grid (though they usually participate in the market primarily for "system cooling" or balancing gas).
- BOTAŞ (Transmission Operator): The state-owned entity that manages the main pipelines and also acts as a major market player.
